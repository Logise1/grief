The Creative Philosophy

GRIEF was designed as an interactive psychological metaphor rather than a traditional platformer. The game translates the emotional phases of the Kübler-Ross model (The Five Stages of Grief) directly into interactive gameplay logic.

The core thesis was to avoid static assets: there are no downloaded sound files, arpeggio samples, or sprites. The atmospheric weather effects, lighting, and ambient musical themes are fully rendered in real-time, matching the player's movements. This procedural approach mirrors the shifting, unpredictable nature of our own interior psychological weather.

THE VOID Intro

"A quiet, heavy emptiness."

Mechanics Rationale

Simple horizontal movement tutorials. Physics are tuned to feel slightly heavy, and the camera spotlight vignette is narrow, representing the shock and emotional numbness that immediately follows tragedy.

Procedural Audio

Absolute silence. The lack of music represents an empty acoustic space, with only the letter-by-letter typing noise of inner thoughts breaking the quiet.

DENIAL Stage 1

"The mind constructs walls to shield itself from reality."

Mechanics Rationale

Glitch & Illusion Platforms: Wireframe platforms only solidify when the player gets within close proximity, and certain thick walls are fake. This forces the player to walk blindly and doubt their senses, mirroring denial's construct of custom truths.

Procedural Audio

A hollow, floating C Minor drone sweep generated by sine oscillators, representing isolation, accompanied by a cold white noise generator simulating sweeping ambient winds.

ANGER Stage 2

"Why me? It's not fair! The world burns with furious flame."

Mechanics Rationale

Fury Dash: The ability to dash forward (`SHIFT`) to shatter red barrier blocks. The level design has high walls, lava pits, and aggressive obstacles. Dashing represents venting rage, breaking barriers, and running with explosive force.

Procedural Audio

Fast, intense synthesizer pulses and heavy distorted rhythmic low-frequency sub-bass beats, paired with explosive white noise bursts simulating lightning strikes and tectonic rumble.

BARGAINING Stage 3

"What if I did things differently? Can we make a deal?"

Mechanics Rationale

Sacrifice & Construct: The player collects golden energy nodes and presses `E` to spend 25% of their energy to spawn temporary golden platforms. The final massive spike gap requires chaining multiple platforms, demonstrating the exhausting transaction of making a deal.

Procedural Audio

Unstable golden FM chords that modulate keys, accompanied by a rhythmic clicking sound produced by high-pass filtered noise ticks, evoking the ticking of a clock and running out of time.

DEPRESSION Stage 4

"A heavy, cold rain. A slow, exhausting crawl through the dark."

Mechanics Rationale

Heavy Physics & Melancholy Walk: Drag is increased, acceleration is cut, and jump height feels sluggish. The level features dark static water and rain. The final stretch is a long, quiet flat march where the player must walk slowly, conveying the heavy exhaustion of grief.

Procedural Audio

A slow, heavy, descending minor-key piano progression generated via sine waves with exponential gain decay. The background plays a persistent rain drone synthesized by bandpass-filtered noise.

ACCEPTANCE Stage 5

"The clouds break. The light is warm, and you can finally breathe."

Mechanics Rationale

Double Jump & Glide: The dark spotlight is lifted, showing the environment. The player gains a double jump and can float/glide by holding Jump, converting the previous heavy gravity into light, graceful flight.

Procedural Audio

A warm C Major 7th chord swell (C, E, G, B) providing ambient drone support. High-frequency bells pluck gently in a C Major Pentatonic scale, conveying sparkling clarity and peace.

HOPE Epilogue

"A quiet, gentle dawn. A path opens into the tomorrow."

Mechanics Rationale

Peaceful Resolution: No hazards, no spikes, and no complex jumps. The spotlight remains absent. The player simply walks across a sunlit grassy field toward a glowing green seedling of light, evoking a sense of renewal, peace, and growth.

Procedural Audio

A C Major 9th chord swell (incorporating a warm D note) layered with an active, uplifting, and steady pentatonic arpeggio arpeggiating upward, capturing the forward-moving energy of recovery.

Behind the Code — Procedural Architecture

Web Audio Synthesis

No external audio assets are loaded. The audio engine uses the browser's AudioContext. Drones are built by summing multiple sine oscillators. Rain and wind are modeled by shaping white noise using a BiquadFilterNode and modulating the filter cutoff over time. Plucks and bells use FM synthesis (modulator oscillator feeding into the carrier frequency) with exponential volume envelopes.

Particle Mood Emitters

The particle engine in particles.js operates on a single canvas, spawning wind-drifted rain, rising ash, drifting fog, and sparkling light cells. Different stages trigger distinct spawners, helping transform the visual aesthetic of the layout.

Optimization Techniques

Traditional lighting mask composites require costly offscreen canvas blending. To maintain a smooth frame rate, we replaced this with direct radial gradients drawn relative to the player's viewport coordinates, running 1000x faster and eliminating GPU lag on mobile and lower-end machines.
